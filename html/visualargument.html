Explanation for High Production Levels

Biennial Production Cycle

  • Arabica coffee makes up 70% of total coffee production
  • These trees have biennial bearing cycle which means they tend to alternate heavy crop and light crop years
  • Causes high coffee production levels every other year
  • Brazil is the world's largeset coffee producer
  • 80% of the Brazil-produced Arabica coffee for 2018/2019 market year came from the regions with trees in their on year

Favorable Temperatures

  • Arabica: optimal growing temperature range 18-21 degrees Celcius
  • Robusta: optimal growing temperature around 20.5 degrees Celcius
  • The graph shows all of the top four producing countries had average temperatures that dropped closer to the optimal temperatures for coffee

Conclusion

The assumption that coffee can grow in any location or any weather condition is wrong. Data on the total production levels and average annual temperature of top coffee producing countries show the Arabica biennial bearing cycle on year and cooler temperatures leads to an increase coffee production.
